provided) 6:20-7:30 Classroom activities with teachers * Curriculum and Developmental Expectations * Community building (family project) * Q&A Mathematics activity Our children have been focusing on numbers within 10. They practice counting, recognizing quantities, and connecting numbers to real objects. One favorite activity involves picking a number, counting out the same number of sticks, and placing them into a jar. This helps children develop one-to-one correspondence and strengthens their fine motor skills. You can also have some simple ways that enhance your child’s math learning: Count fruits, toys, or steps together, lay board games or card games that involve numbers Mathematics game- Who has bigger number This week, our children also played a game called “Bigger Number Eats Smaller Number.” Children were working in pairs, each child randomly picked a card and placed it on the table. The child with the larger number “ate” the smaller number. This activity helped reinforce number recognition and comparison skills. Sample text Origami activity This week, our children also participated in an origami activity, which provided another opportunity to practise eye-hand coordination and strengthen their fine motor skills. Fish Tank Do you know that we have a fish tank in our class? It was given by the previous class. The children noticed that there are two small fish, a 'castle'', some plants and rocks in it. Amy, Skyler's mom who kindly offered to help cleaning our fish tank. This Thursday, Amy led some children to pumped some water out, and gave the fresh water treatment to clear the fluoride. Some of the children helped to test the level of water's PH. ammonia, nitrite and nitrate. Some helped to cut pieces of Ammonia- Reducing filter pads. We are very happy that the water now is clean and healthy for the fish and excited to have more aquatic creatures to join the fish tank.
